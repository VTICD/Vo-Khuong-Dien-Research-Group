Vo Khuong Dien Research Group

Theoretical & Computational Research on Excitons in 2D Quantum Materials

Our group focuses on exciton physics, light–matter interactions, valleytronics, and thermoelectric transport in low-dimensional materials using first-principles and many-body methods.

Research

Exciton Physics in 2D Materials

This work investigates how crystal-symmetry breaking in monolayer SnS and SnSe enables robust valley-selective optical transitions. Using first-principles GW-BSE calculations, the study reveals that different polarization states of light couple selectively to distinct electronic valleys, providing a controllable valley degree of freedom. The results identify tin chalcogenide monolayers as promising candidates for next-generation valleytronic devices where information can be encoded and manipulated through valley polarization.

  • Nguyen Thanh Tien, Pham Thi Bich Thao, Nguyen Thi Han, Vo Khuong Dien. “Symmetry-driven valleytronics in single-layer tin chalcogenides SnS and SnSe.” PRB 109, 155416 (2024). DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevB.109.155416

Thermoelectric & Lattice Thermal Transport

Using first-principles electron–phonon coupling and phonon anharmonicity, we explore ways to tune lattice thermal conductivity and ZT in low-dimensional materials.

  • Penta-graphene and penta-XP5 (X = Al, Ga, In).
  • Defect, doping, and strain engineering for ZT enhancement.
  • Interplay between excitons, phonons, and transport properties.

Valleytronics & Topological Phenomena

We analyze Berry curvature, valley-resolved band structures, and spin–orbit induced splittings for potential valleytronic device applications.

  • Valley-selective optical selection rules and spin–valley locking.
  • Symmetry breaking (substrates, strain, doping) and topological transitions.
  • Plumbene/hBN and related heterostructures.

Methods & Software Development

We develop Python/Fortran tools around packages such as VASP, Quantum ESPRESSO, Wannier90, EPW, and WannierBSE to automate workflows for band structures, Berry curvature, excitons, and transport.

  • Non-uniform k-mesh generation and unfolding.
  • Post-processing for spin textures and Berry curvature maps.
  • Teaching-oriented scripts for students to learn quickly.

Teaching

The lab is actively involved in teaching and curriculum development in physics, semiconductor devices, and automotive systems.

Introduction to Automotive Systems (AISI101)

Fundamentals of vehicle structure, powertrain, braking, steering, and automotive electronics, with an emphasis on system-level thinking.

Quantum Simulation & Computational Materials Design

Hands-on introduction to DFT/DFPT, Wannier90, EPW, and BSE methods for 2D materials, including scripting in Python/Matlab.

Condensed Matter Physics / Solid State Physics

Band theory, phonons, transport, and modern topics such as Berry curvature and topological materials.

Special Topics: Excitons in Low-Dimensional Systems

Advanced topics in many-body perturbation theory and exciton physics, aimed at graduate students and motivated undergraduates.
